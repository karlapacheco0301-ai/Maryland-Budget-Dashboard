Budget Description
FY2027 · All Funds · Source: Maryland Operating Budget
The FY2027 Maryland Operating Budget totals $64.9 billion across all fund types — the fourth consecutive year where the General Fund is smaller than the prior year, reflecting the Moore-Miller administration's commitment to fiscal responsibility without sacrificing core services.
The budget is funded by three major sources: General Funds ($27.8B, 42.9%) derived from state taxes; Federal Funds ($21.1B, 32.6%) from congressional appropriations; and Special Funds ($15.9B, 24.5%) from dedicated revenue streams such as transportation fees and higher education tuition.
Critical context: The Trump-Vance administration is withholding federally appropriated funds, placing 32.6% of Maryland's total budget at structural risk. The state has implemented contingency measures including $43M in DHS funding pick-up and $13M in new General Fund obligations.

About This Analysis

This Risk Payment Intelligence module analyzes $2.47B in annual risk obligations across Maryland State government using FY2019–FY2024 historical data from the Maryland CAFR, SRPS Annual Report, State Employee & Retiree Health & Welfare Benefits Program (SERHWB), and Operating Budget data.

Methodology: Linear, exponential, and Monte Carlo forecast models applied to each category independently. Confidence intervals reflect actuarial uncertainty ranges published in official reports.

Executive Summary — Maryland Risk Intelligence FY2024

Maryland's total risk payment obligations of $2.47B annually represent a 3.8% compound annual growth rate since FY2019. The risk portfolio is dominated by Group Health Insurance at 85% of total spend ($2.1B), leaving the State financially exposed to health cost inflation and demographic trends in its workforce.

The Self-Insurance Fund deficit of $180M is the most urgent near-term risk. This fund, meant to buffer against catastrophic claims, is underwater and deteriorating at $42M per year — threatening the State's ability to self-fund against large loss events.

The SRPS pension system at 68% funded ratio is below the 80% benchmark adopted by most actuarial standards, creating a long-term structural obligation that will require sustained contributions of $1.8B+ annually for the next decade.

Action Required: Three interventions could save $185–280M over 4 years: (1) SERHWB plan redesign, (2) Self-Insurance Fund recapitalization, (3) Workers' Comp prevention program. Without action, FY2029 risk spend reaches $2.98B (P50) under base case assumptions.

4. Taxes

Individual Income Taxes

iFile (interactive2.marylandtaxes.gov)

State income tax e-filing portal. TY2025 version active. Used by millions of Maryland filers. Separate system from Maryland Tax Connect (business). Separate login from all other state portals.

Comptroller — state-built E-filing / submission Citizen portal ⚠ overlap

Maryland Tax Connect

New self-service portal for business taxpayers, launched Feb 2024. Handles sales tax, withholding, corporate returns. Expanding to all taxpayers. Separate from iFile — parallel system.

Comptroller — state-built Citizen portal E-filing / submission Payment ⚠ overlap

IRS Direct File (federal integration)

Maryland joined for TY2024/2025 filing seasons. 700K+ eligible Marylanders. Federal IRS platform with MD state integration layer for state return completion.

IRS (federal) E-filing / submission Citizen portal

Where's My Refund Portal

Individual refund status lookup on Comptroller portal. Real-time return processing status. Separate authenticated tool within marylandcomptroller.gov.

Comptroller — state-built Citizen portal Reporting / analytics

Integrated Tax System (ITS) MITDP

Comptroller + DoIT (development) Infrastructure / platform

6. Certificates and public records

MDH MDEVRRS · VitalChek · NAPHSIS · Tyler MDEC · RowNet

Vital Records — Birth, Death, Marriage (MDH)

MDEVRRS (MD Electronic Vital Records Registration System)

4 integrated systems: MDEBRS (births), MDEDRS (deaths), MDFDRS (fetal deaths), MDVRBIS (BI analytics). UC Davis Cal-IVRS codebase. Deployed on AWS within MD-THINK. Built 2019–present by UC San Diego.

AWS (MD-THINK) · UC San Diego Registry / records Reporting / analytics Infrastructure / platform

VitalChek (online ordering)

Sole authorized online vendor for Maryland certified birth/death/marriage certificate orders. LexisNexis subsidiary. $10–$30 per certificate. Orders fulfilled by MDH Division of Vital Records.

VitalChek / LexisNexis Citizen portal Payment ⚠ overlap

EVVE — Electronic Verification of Vital Events

NAPHSIS national real-time system. Authorized agencies (SSA, passport, benefits) verify birth/death data directly against MDEVRRS. No PII stored. Encrypted real-time query/response.

NAPHSIS (Silver Spring, MD) Identity / verification Infrastructure / platform

STEVE — State/Territorial Exchange of Vital Events

Interstate vital records data sharing. Maryland shares birth/death data with other states when events occur outside resident state. Also NAPHSIS-operated companion to EVVE.

NAPHSIS Registry / records Infrastructure / platform

MVA Vital Records Integration (Oct 2024)

MVA branches print certified birth and death certificates on-site ($10) via MDH-MDEVRRS integration. Expanded June 2025. Two-agent security model for privacy.

MDOT MVA + MDH integration Registry / records Identity / verification

Court, Estate, and Public Records

MDEC Odyssey Case Search Portal

Public case lookup, docket access, court document retrieval across all Maryland courts. New Maryland Judiciary Case Search & Record Portal launched March 14, 2026.

Tyler Technologies (Odyssey) Registry / records Citizen portal

Registers of Wills — RowNet

Online estate record search across all 24 Maryland jurisdictions. Public probate filings, inventory, and account lookup at registers.maryland.gov/RowNetWeb.

State-built (Registers of Wills) Registry / records Citizen portal

Maryland Land Records (mdlandrec.net)

Online index and image retrieval for deeds, mortgages, liens, and plats. Used for property title searches, chain-of-title research, and MPIA public records access.

State-built (Maryland Archives) Registry / records Citizen portal

Cross-cutting platform: MD-THINK (Amazon Web Services)

Maryland Total Human-services Integrated Network — first-in-nation multi-agency cloud platform. Built by Maryland DHS with Deloitte on AWS (2017–present). FedRAMP compliant. Runs on EC2, RDS, ECS, EBS, EFS in AWS GovCloud East Region 1.

CJAMS — Child, Juvenile & Adult Management

Unified case management for child welfare, juvenile justice, and adult protective services. iPad-based field data entry across all 24 counties + Baltimore City.

AWS / MD-THINK · Deloitte Case management Infrastructure / platform

One Application (July 2025)

Mobile-first unified benefits intake for Medicaid, SNAP, TANF, WIC, Energy Assistance. Single form replacing agency-by-agency in-person visits. Built by DoIT MDDS on MD-THINK.

MD-THINK / DoIT MDDS Citizen portal E-filing / submission ⚠ overlap

Unified Benefits Screener (Dec 2024)

Pre-application eligibility checker for 5 safety-net programs. Maryland's first interagency online tool. Feeds into One Application flow. Replaces program-by-program manual screening.

MD-THINK / DoIT MDDS Citizen portal Reporting / analytics

MDEVRRS on MD-THINK

Maryland's 4 electronic vital records systems (MDEBRS, MDEDRS, MDFDRS, MDVRBIS) all deployed within MD-THINK AWS. Migrated 80+ years of legacy birth data during implementation.

AWS (MD-THINK) · UC San Diego Registry / records Infrastructure / platform
